Contact and Bio

Miguel Cardenas-Arroyo

Miguel Cardenas-Arroyo (he/him) is a Latin-American writer, aspiring academic librarian and archivist from Los Angeles now living in New York City. They are a recent alum from NYU's XE: Experimental Humanities & Social Engagement program and are currently studying Library and Information Sciences with an advanced certificate in Archives at Pratt Institute. Their research interests are in queer and feminist practices, post-Marxist theory, and experimental writing. Their current work focuses on the interactions among Latine literature, queerness, memory, the body, and relations to home through experimental narratives. Cardenas-Arroyo views language as a catalyst for change and uncovers their truth through the act of writing.

Their favorite anime is Frieren: Beyond Journey's End because it looks into the “everyday” as a thousand-year-old elven mage realizes the beauty of connection. Their coffee order is four shots of blonde espresso with coconut milk. Cardenas-Arroyo is a Sagittarius (to a fault) and is a self-proclaimed French fry and ranch sommelier.
